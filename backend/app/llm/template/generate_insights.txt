Write a concise plain-text insight of 4–6 sentences. Rules:

1. If the user asked a question, answer it directly and specifically in the first sentence.
   Do not open with "The dataset shows…" or "Based on the analysis…"

2. Lead with the single most surprising or actionable finding — not the most obvious one.
   "Surprising" means: an inversion of intuition, a disproportionate concentration,
   an unexpected correlation, or a segment that behaves differently from the rest.

3. Quantify claims with orders of magnitude, not raw numbers.
   GOOD: "roles in the top salary quartile show roughly half the AI risk of bottom-quartile roles"
   BAD:  "the correlation coefficient is -0.244759"

4. If follow-up findings were provided and they stress-test or qualify the main finding,
   incorporate the qualification naturally — e.g. "however, this pattern does not hold in…"

5. End with one concrete, actionable implication for the user.
   GOOD: "This suggests that upskilling into higher-paying specialisations may be a practical hedge against automation risk."
   BAD:  "Overall, the analysis highlights the importance of considering AI risk."

Do NOT use bullet points. Do NOT repeat raw column names verbatim.
Do NOT write generic filler sentences that would apply to any dataset.
