## How to decide what to follow up on

Scan the first-pass results and look for exactly one of these four patterns:

**1. A sentiment finding that raises a cross-analysis question**
   e.g. overall sentiment is 62% positive → "Does this hold across all rating tiers,
   or is one tier dragging the average down?"
   e.g. topic cluster X has lowest doc_count → "Are the few documents in that cluster
   disproportionately negative compared to the dominant clusters?"

**2. A topic or keyword that deserves deeper segmentation**
   e.g. "fast delivery" is the top keyword → "Do texts mentioning delivery have
   significantly different sentiment than those that don't?"
   e.g. topic cluster "product quality" → "Which specific words within this cluster
   correlate with negative sentiment?"

**3. A dimension completely absent from pass 1**
   Pass 1 looked at overall sentiment → Pass 2 could look at:
   - Sentiment vs text length (do longer texts express more nuanced/negative sentiment?)
   - Outlier documents (which specific texts are sentiment extremes, and what do they say?)
   - Temporal patterns if a date column exists
   Rule: do NOT re-examine any (metric × grouping) pair already computed in pass 1.

**4. A claim worth stress-testing in a subgroup**
   Pass 1 found "reviews are mostly positive" → does this hold for:
   - A specific category or product type?
   - Texts above median length only?
   - A specific date range?

Pick whichever pattern gives the most actionable or surprising follow-up.
If none applies — the data is ordinary — return an empty result.
